# Assi Ghat to Sarnath by Scooty - Complete Route Guide

Sarnath, the place where Buddha first taught the Dharma, is one of the four most important Buddhist pilgrimage sites. Located just 12 km from Assi Ghat, it's a perfect half-day trip on a rented scooty.

## Quick Facts

- **Distance:** 12-13 km from Assi Ghat
- **Time:** 25-30 minutes ride
- **Best Route:** Via BHU and Sarnath Road
- **Fuel Needed:** ~1 liter (₹100-110)
- **Best Time:** 8 AM - 12 PM (before heat)
- **Total Trip Duration:** 4-5 hours including visit

## The Route: Assi Ghat to Sarnath

### Route Option 1: Via BHU (Recommended)

**Total Distance:** 13 km
**Estimated Time:** 28 minutes
**Road Quality:** Excellent

1. Start from Assi Ghat
2. Head towards BHU Main Gate (2 km)
3. Enter BHU campus, follow signs for Lanka Gate
4. Exit from Lanka Gate
5. Take Sarnath Road (NH 19)
6. Follow signs for Sarnath

**Why this route?**
- Better road condition
- Scenic BHU campus
- Less traffic
- Clear signage

### Route Option 2: Direct via Cantt Road

**Total Distance:** 12 km
**Estimated Time:** 25 minutes
**Road Quality:** Good

1. Assi → Sigra
2. Sigra → Cantt via Maldahiya
3. Cantt → Sarnath Road (NH 19)

**Less scenic but slightly faster**

## Detailed Riding Instructions

### From Assi Ghat (Starting Point)

**Landmarks to watch:**
- Durga Temple (left side after 1 km)
- BHU Main Gate (right side at 2 km)
- Vishwanath Temple BHU (inside campus)

### Through BHU Campus

- Speed limit: 30 km/hr
- Watch for students crossing
- Beautiful tree-lined roads
- Takes 7-10 minutes to cross

### Sarnath Road (NH 19)

- Well-maintained 4-lane highway
- Straight road, easy riding
- Light traffic usually
- Speed limit: 60 km/hr (stay at 40-45)

### Entering Sarnath

- Look for "Sarnath Archaeological Site" signs
- Main parking area clearly marked
- Parking: ₹10-20 for scootys

## What to See at Sarnath

### Main Attractions (Walking Distance)

1. **Dhamek Stupa** (5 mins walk)
   - 128 feet tall
   - Built 5th century CE
   - Entry: ₹20 (Indians), ₹200 (Foreigners)

2. **Mulagandha Kuti Vihar** (Adjacent)
   - Modern temple with beautiful frescoes
   - Free entry
   - Remove shoes before entering

3. **Sarnath Archaeological Museum** (10 mins walk)
   - Famous Ashoka Pillar Lion Capital
   - Closed on Fridays
   - Entry: ₹20
   - Photography not allowed inside

4. **Ashoka Pillar** (5 mins walk)
   - Original pillar site
   - Inscription visible
   - Included in monument ticket

5. **Thai Temple** (10 mins walk)
   - Beautiful Thai architecture
   - Peaceful atmosphere
   - Free entry

### Time Required

- Quick visit: 2 hours
- Moderate visit: 3 hours
- Detailed with guide: 4 hours

## Tips for Sarnath Scooty Trip

### Before You Leave

✅ Fill fuel (ensure tank is at least 50%)
✅ Carry water bottle
✅ Sunscreen and sunglasses
✅ Phone fully charged (GPS)
✅ Helmet (provided with rental)
✅ Small bag for temple shoe removal

### During the Ride

✅ Follow GPS but watch road signs too
✅ Stick to speed limits
✅ Take NH 19 (smoother road)
✅ Stop at BHU if needed (restrooms available)
✅ Watch for speed breakers in Sarnath area

### At Sarnath

✅ Park in designated paid parking only
✅ Lock your scooty properly
✅ Don't leave valuables in scooty
✅ Guides available (₹300-500)
✅ Dress modestly (temples)
✅ Remove shoes at temples

## Fuel & Parking

### Fuel Stations on Route

1. **Near BHU** - Indian Oil (2 km from Assi)
2. **Cantt Area** - Multiple options
3. **Before Sarnath** - HP Petrol Pump (10 km mark)

### Parking at Sarnath

- **Main Parking:** Near Dhamek Stupa (₹10-20)
- **Temple Parking:** Thai Temple has free parking
- **Safe:** Well-maintained with security

## Food Options at Sarnath

### Near Monuments

- Small cafés and tea stalls
- Budget: ₹50-100 per person
- Limited options, better to eat back in Varanasi

### Recommended

- Have breakfast in Assi before leaving
- Light snacks at Sarnath
- Proper lunch back in Varanasi

## Safety Tips

1. **Helmet Always:** Mandatory on highways
2. **Stay Left:** Let faster vehicles pass
3. **No Rush:** Smooth, steady riding
4. **Check Weather:** Avoid if heavy rain predicted
5. **Emergency Contact:** Save our 24/7 helpline

## Best Time to Visit

### By Season

- **October-March:** Best weather
- **April-June:** Very hot, go early morning
- **July-September:** Monsoon, roads may be slippery

### By Day

- **Early Morning (7-9 AM):** Cool, less crowd
- **Late Afternoon (4-6 PM):** Good light for photos
- **Avoid:** 11 AM - 3 PM (peak heat)

## Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Going during 12-2 PM (too hot)
❌ Not carrying water
❌ Rushing the ride (enjoy the journey)
❌ Forgetting entry fee cash
❌ Not locking scooty properly
❌ Skipping museum (has best artifacts)
